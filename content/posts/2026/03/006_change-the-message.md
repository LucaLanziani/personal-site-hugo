Please Stop Force Feeding the AI FOMO on others!

Not too long ago I wrote a post about the [AI Hype Burnout](https://ainativeteams.com/blog/ai-hype-effects/) and how it was harmful to the whole industry. I thought that was the end of it, but it seems like the FOMO is starting to kick in. I see more and more people talking how finally AI is delivering real value and how we should all jump on the bandwagon.

<!--more-->

I get it, it's exciting and I'm excited too, but please, let's not force feed the FOMO on others.

Just yesterday I heard the phrase "The developers not using AI will be left behind" in a conversation. I understand what the person wanted to say, but it came across as a threat. A more pragmatic message would be: 

> AI is currently accelerating developers who know how to use it effectively, it doesn't mean that those who don't use it will be left behind, but they might miss out on some of the benefits and efficiencies that AI can provide. Thankfully AI is not rocket science, it can be picked up quite quickly, it just requires some iterations to get the hang of it. So if you're not using AI yet, don't worry, just start experimenting with it and you'll see how it can help you in your work.

Ofcourse this will not work with people that are rejecting AI all together, but for those that are on the fence, this is a much more positive message that encourages them to try it out without feeling like they're being left behind.

This is not as catchy as "Don't be left behind, start using AI now!" and I would love to have a catchy phrase to share a more positive message.

If you have any ideas, please share them with me.
